\chapter*{Conclusion}

\vspace{20pt}

{\fontsize{14}{18}\selectfont\color{slate}\itshape
From the first sacrifice in Eden to the final Lamb upon the throne, one voice echoes through Scripture: redemption belongs to God alone.\par}

\vspace{20pt}

Leviticus is not a catalog of rituals but a revelation of Christ---the Holy One who calls sinners near, the High Priest who enters once for all, the Sacrifice that perfects forever those who draw near by faith.

\begin{scripture}
``Therefore He is also able to save to the uttermost those who come to God through Him, since He always lives to make intercession for them.''
\scriptureref{Hebrews 7:25}
\end{scripture}

Every offering whispered His name. Every priest pointed to His priesthood. Every feast anticipated His fulfillment. The veil has been torn, the way opened, the blood applied.

Now we come boldly to the throne of grace, not by merit but by mercy---not by works but by the finished work of the Lamb slain from the foundation of the world.

\vspace{30pt}

{\color{accent}\rule{3in}{2pt}}

\vspace{20pt}

{\fontsize{12}{16}\selectfont\scshape\color{gray}
Soli Deo Gloria\par}
